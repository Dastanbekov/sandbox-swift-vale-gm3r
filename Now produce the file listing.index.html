About the ICPC

The International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC) is the oldest, largest, and most prestigious programming contest in the world. Founded in 1977, the ICPC has grown to include over 3,000 universities from more than 100 countries.

Teams of three students work together to solve real-world algorithmic problems under a time limit. The contest fosters creativity, teamwork, and innovation in computer science.

History

The first ICPC was held in 1977 at the University of Texas at Austin. Since then, it has expanded globally, with regional contests on every continent. The World Finals bring together the best teams to compete for the championship title.

Registration

Secure your spot today! Registration is open to teams of three students from accredited universities.

  • Each team must have a coach (faculty member).
  • Participants must be enrolled in a degree program.
  • Registration fee: varies by region.
